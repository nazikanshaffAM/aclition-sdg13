Understanding Climate Change

What is Climate Change?
Climate change refers to shift in weather conditions over time.
Climate change is regarded as one of the most profound issues regarding the environment
The impacts of Climate Change are said to be long-term.
Many expert profusely highlight that the effects are very likely to be irreversible.
Causes of Climate Change
Modern day activities have led to a rise in atmospheric levels of greenhouse gases i.e. methan, nitrous oxide, carbon monoxide etc.
Common examples include:
  • Burning of fossil fuels
  • Deforestation
  • Poor waste management systems
Realities of Climate Change
Rise in global sea surface temperatures by 1.5 °C since 1900
Global carbon dioxide (CO2) concentration is now around 424 parts per million (highest recorded in over 2 million years)
An average increase in Australia's climate by 1.5 °C since 1910s (Warmest temperature recorded during this period was in 1950)
